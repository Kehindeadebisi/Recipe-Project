My Favorite Meal is Jollof Rice

About Jollof Rice

Also known as benachin and thieboudienne, jollof rice is a one-pot meal that originated from the Wolof tribe in the 14th century.
Wolof Empire, ruling parts of modern-day Senegal, The Gambia and Mauritania, popularized jollof throughout West Africa.

Although jollof rice was traditionally cooked with fish for dinner, Africans today also enjoy this savory dish for breakfast and lunch,
often substitute fish with chicken, beef or goat.

Each country has added its own spin to the recipe and West Africans engage in humorous banter over who makes the best jollof.
friendly rivalries, known as the “Jollof Wars,” have become an African cultural phenomenon.

Materials Needed

Ingredients Measurements
Rice 2 Cups
Pepper Mix 1 Cup
Onion 1 Bulb
Tomato Paste 1 Satchet
Groundut Oil 2 Tablespoons
Spices 1 Tea Spoon
Salt To taste

Steps to Prepare Jollof Rice

  • Perboil the rice and set it aside
  • Heat the Groundut oil
  • Add the onions and fry for a few minutes
  • Add the tomato paste and stir till it dissolves
  • Add the pepper mix and allow it to cook for about 15 minutes
  • Add spices and salt to taste and cover till the peeper mix is well cooked
  • Add water and leave till it boils
  • Add your rice and cover till it is soft
